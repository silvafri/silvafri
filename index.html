Campaigns

CU Denver Free Menstrual Products Campaign



This research report is one part of the larger CU Denver Free Menstrual Menstrual Campaign.

Part of our campaign was to create a research report that included both quantitative and qualitative data that supported our rationale and purpose for advocating for free menstrual products at our institution. This report was presented to higher administration including the chancellor. At the end of the semester, she gave us the green-light for implementation for the following academic year.

A student and I worked on the the Free Menstrual Product campaign that recognized the need to establish one of the basic necessities of female, non-binary and transgender health, free menstrual products.

The campaign promoted the health and academic focus of these students by alleviating some of the financial stresses that come with menstruating by providing free menstrual products in CU Denver restrooms. Higher administration is developing a ten-year wellness plan to be a leading university in promoting “urban university wellness.”

Our campaign created a background report and proposal that urged the chancellor to approve free menstrual products as a reoccurring line item in the university’s budget by demonstrating the alignment between our campaign and the university’s priority to be a leading school in supporting student health and wellness. After months of lobbying, petitioning, we submitted our proposal and managed to secure around $14,183 for a pilot program that would dispense free menstrual products in high traffic restrooms in each CU Denver building.

Outreach

Tivoli Tenants Festival Tabling Session

Although it is more efficient to reach our commuter student population through social media, we make efforts to establish long lasting impressions through our interpersonal outreach. Interpersonal outreach is a large component of how we establish trust through genuine discussions about sorority and fraternity life and the model CU Denver has planned for the students. One of our most effective and most engaging forms of outreach are our tabling sessions around campus. We table biweekly in different high-traffic CU Denver spaces to maximize exposure and engagement.

Before a tabling session, I plan our purpose for tabling and our end goal: get students to sign up on an email list, increase our social media following, provide sorority and fraternity take aways, etc.

The images on slide 8 and 9 are from a tabling session we had during a welcome week festival at school. We wanted to increase our program’s exposure by providing CU Denver SFL SWAG. In order for students to get SWAG, we simply asked them to write on our white board what they thought when they heard “Sorority and Fraternity Life.” This in turn, gave us a general idea of the sentiments our students have about SFL to tailor our communications.

Branding and Market Analysis



During my time as Legal Intern at Molson Coors, I worked closely with the Corporate Affairs team. The head of VP for the department gave me a project regarding the research and application of corporate branding at Molson Coors.

Without clear instruction, I picked the brains of a few of the corporate affair’s team members to develop a plan for my research. After receiving the team’s insight, I focused my research on a corporate brand analysis analyzing Molson Coors and top companies’ corporate brand to inform how to reenergize and modernize the brand of Molson Coors.

During my research I actually analyzed Microsoft’s corporate brand as an example of exceptional leadership that sets missions and values that then inform an organization culture that is welcoming, productive, and high-achieving.
